CarbonQt is an interactive Linux desktop application designed to monitor, visualize, and reduce the carbon footprint of computational workloads. Inspired by the open-source library **CodeCarbon**, CarbonQt transforms backend emission tracking into an elegant **Qt-based real-time GUI experience** for developers, researchers, and system administrators.

---

## 🌍 **Project Overview**

Modern computing—especially AI, simulation, and high-performance workloads—consumes significant energy and contributes to global CO₂ emissions. Yet, most users have *no visibility* into how their systems consume power.

**CarbonQt solves this.**

It provides a real-time, intuitive platform that:

* Tracks **system energy usage**
* Estimates **CO₂ emissions**
* Displays **per-process & per-thread power consumption**
* Monitors **CPU context switches** and their energy overhead
* Allows monitoring of specific apps or directories
* Suggests environmentally optimal times to run heavy workloads via a **Green Mode**

CarbonQt bridges energy awareness with actionable insights, enabling sustainable computing practices for everyday users.

| 5   | Implement **Green Mode** for low-carbon-intensity scheduling                     |

## 📘 **Project Storyline**

As computational workloads grow—driven by AI, big data, and real-time systems—the energy footprint of software becomes a critical concern. Developers rarely realize how their algorithms and environments contribute to CO₂ emissions.

**CarbonQt was envisioned to make these invisible impacts visible.**

It translates raw system metrics into intuitive visualizations so users can:

* Understand their real-time environmental impact
* Optimize when and how they run applications
* Adopt sustainable computing habits
* Use energy insights for research and optimization

By merging Qt’s UI power with low-level Linux metrics, CarbonQt becomes both a **practical tool** and an **educational instrument** for green computing.
